Philip & Sher’s event was simply a dinner celebration of their love. Having been already wed in the US, they decided to come home to the Philippines and gather their families and friends for a reception. Sure, we didn’t spend a whole day with them from preps to ceremony and then to reception, but the short time we were able to spend with them is one we will treasure forever.

It’s not every day that we encounter people who do not speak the same language as we do, yet we had that unique experience with Howard. Able to speak fluent Chinese, we thought it would be a challenge to communicate with him. However, we were pleasantly surprised with how easily we could work together. Since their engagement shoot up until their wedding day, we felt his full trust, together with his lovely bride Nicole, who translated for him

Kyle & Eunica brought our team to Burnham Grove Estate in Sydney, Australia to capture their, and dare we say it, PERFECT wedding. It was so refreshing to be part of a such a chill, no drama, no pressure wedding from the beginning to end.

Sarah’s tinghun was distinctly beautiful, with a quiet sense of certainty. Just look at their portraits and you’ll know what I mean. And Sarah in her white gown – isn’t it splendid? I don’t think anybody can pull off such a dress, but she did so with so much ease. Love this tinghun! Enjoy the photos!

Take for instance, Brian & Angela’s tinghun. While it preserves the time-honored traditions and practices around the Chinese engagement ceremony, we saw a lot of variations that show just how unique each couple and each family are.

We are closing 2018 soon, but not without sharing this wonderful tinghun ceremony Kim, and their respective families. It’s classic and elegant, but also relaxed and warm, something characteristic about tinghuns
